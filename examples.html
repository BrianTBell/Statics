1. Equilibrium Conditions

Equilibrium conditions are fundamental in statics, ensuring a rigid body remains at rest or moves with constant velocity. They require that all forces acting on the body balance, preventing any net movement, and that all rotational effects, or moments, cancel out, stopping any rotation. Moments are the turning effects caused by forces applied at a distance from a point. These principles are essential for analyzing stable structures like bridges, beams, or trusses in engineering design.
